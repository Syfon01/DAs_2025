Sifon Isaac

I’ve never been the “let’s see” type of guy. Even when life felt uncertain, I knew I wanted something intentional and meaningful in life ( career, relationship, friendships). That clarity kept me focused on becoming a better version of myself rather than rushing into relationships. When I eventually fell in love with Ifure, it came with so much peace, friendship, and a sense of certainty I didn’t have to force. Being with someone who brings balance, supports my growth, and makes life feel steady has been one of my biggest blessings. I’m grateful for a partnership built on clarity, respect, and quiet confidence. The kind of love that feels both grounding and purposeful.

Ifure Iwuoenang

I may be uncertain about some things in life, but I have always been sure of what I wanted in a man. I knew I didn’t want “vibes and Insha Allah,” and that clarity kept me away from the dating scene for a long time. In the university, my focus was clear; to excel in my studies, make a meaningful impact in my community, and deepen my relationship with God. So, no matter how appealing relationships seemed around me then, I wasn’t interested. After reading Sex Demystified by Christie Bature at 15, I desired something different, intentional, and purposeful. At 16, I wrote in my diary that I would only fall in love after NYSC, when I felt ready, but God had other plans. I met Sifon while I was in the university, we built a solid friendship which blossomed into a relationship. Sifon is my safe space, a disciplined man whom I deeply respect, and I am truly grateful to be doing life with him.
