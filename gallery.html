Sifon Isaac

I’ve never been the “let’s see how it goes” type of guy. Even when life felt uncertain, I knew I wanted something intentional and meaningful in life ( career, relationship, friendships). That awareness kept me focused on becoming a better version of myself rather than rushing into relationships. When I eventually fell in love with my darling Ifure, it came with so much peace, friendship, and a sense of certainty I didn’t have to force. Being with someone who brings balance, supports my growth, and makes life feel steady has been one of my biggest blessings. I’m grateful for a partnership built on clarity, respect, and most especially Godly principles. The love we share feels both grounding and purposeful, and I'm eternally glad God helped me pitch my tent here.

Ifure Iwuoenang

I may be uncertain about some things in life, but I have always been sure of what I wanted in a man. I knew I didn’t want “vibes and Insha Allah,” and that clarity kept me away from the dating scene for a long time. In the university, my focus was clear; to excel in my studies, make a meaningful impact in my community, and deepen my relationship with God. So, no matter how appealing relationships seemed around me then, I wasn’t interested. Also, after reading "Sex Demystified" by Christie Bature at 15, I desired something different, intentional, and purposeful. In my year 1, I wrote in my diary that I would only fall in love after NYSC, but God had other plans because I met Sifon while I was in the university. We built a solid friendship which blossomed into a relationship. Sifon has grown to become my safe space, a disciplined man whom I deeply respect, and I am grateful to be doing life with him.
